Efficient use of energy — Entertainment & Electronics: Desktop vs. Laptop
Level: BASIC
Topic Computing — desktop vs. laptop energy use
Learning outcomes
  • Compare energy usage of computing devices.
  • Analyse efficiency vs. performance.
  • Calculate operating costs over a year.
Time needed45–60 min
Resources needed
  • Device specifications (from manufacturer label or website)
  • Calculator

Introduction

Computers are essential for entertainment, learning and work, but their energy consumption varies greatly between desktops and laptops. In this worksheet you will look up the power rating of two devices, calculate their annual energy use and cost, and compare what a school could save by switching from desktops to laptops.

Did you know?

Laptops typically use 60–80 % less energy than desktop computers. They’re designed to run from a battery, so every component — CPU, screen, drives — is engineered for low-power operation. Even when plugged in, that efficiency stays with you.

Reflection

When is a desktop still the better choice despite higher energy use? Think about gaming, video editing, scientific computing, server roles, repair-friendliness, and total lifetime.
