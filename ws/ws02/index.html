Worksheet 02

Standby vs. OFF — Hidden Energy Consumption
Level: BASIC
Topic 01: Efficient Use of Energy — Entertainment & Electronics
Learning outcomes
  • Identify standby (“vampire”) energy consumption.
  • Compare power usage in different device states.
  • Calculate annual energy waste and its cost.
Time needed 45–60 min
Resources needed TV / console / monitor specifications (or internet), calculator, power meter (optional).

Introduction

Many electronic devices continue to consume energy even when they are “turned off” — as long as they remain plugged in. This is called standby power, or more colourfully, vampire energy. A small LED, a clock display, or a wireless receiver waiting for a remote signal all draw a few watts continuously.

Individually it seems trivial. Across a household — TVs, game consoles, monitors, chargers, microwaves — it adds up to a noticeable share of the electricity bill, and to wasted carbon emissions. In this worksheet you will quantify exactly how much is wasted and what it costs.

Annual energy formula

The formula below converts continuous power draw into yearly energy use. For standby, assume the device sits in standby for 20 hours per day.

$$ E_{\,\text{kWh}} = \dfrac{P_{\,\text{W}} \times h \times 365}{1000} $$
E = annual energy (kWh) P = device power (W) h = hours per day in that state

💡 Did you know?

Turning off devices at the wall socket — instead of leaving them on standby — can save up to 10 % of household electricity. A switched power strip lets you cut several devices at once with a single flick.
