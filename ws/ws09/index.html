Worksheet 09

Renewable Energy — Solar Potential of our School
Level: ADVANCED
Topic Renewable Energy — Solar Potential of our School
Learning outcomes
  • Technical knowledge: Students learn about solar PV systems, orientation, and how geographic location affects energy yield.
  • Mathematical skills: Students calculate potential energy production (kWh) based on surface area (m²) and solar irradiance.
  • Environmental awareness: Students understand the role of renewables in reducing carbon footprints and the seasonal nature of energy in the North.
  • Green skills: Critical thinking regarding the feasibility and ROI (Return on Investment) of green technology.
Time needed 60–90 min
Resources needed Internet access, Google Maps (for roof area), PVGIS Tool or Renewables.ninja, and a calculator.

Introduction

To become more sustainable, schools can look beyond saving energy and start producing their own. By utilising unused roof space for solar panels (photovoltaics), we can reduce our reliance on the grid. This audit uses satellite data to estimate how much “green” energy our roof can generate.

Activities and tasks for students

ASite Analysis & Surface Area

  • Locate the school: use Google Maps (Satellite view) to find the school roof.
  • Estimate area: measure a suitable section of the roof (e.g. south-facing).
Estimated roof area: m²
  • Solar panel efficiency: assume 1 m² of solar panels produces roughly 200 W (0.2 kW) in peak conditions.
Potential system size: kWp (kilowatt-peak)

Peer-to-peer tip

💡 Did you know?

Solar panels work more efficiently in cold temperatures. If there is sun and no snow covering them, a cold climate is actually a plus for panel efficiency!

Reflection

Compare the energy production of the summer months vs. the winter months. How does this seasonality affect the school’s “green” goals, and does it change your mind about the importance of saving energy during the darkest months?
